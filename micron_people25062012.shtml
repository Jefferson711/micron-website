Micron-associated research groups

There are around a dozen core Micron-associated research groups based across several Oxford departments. These groups interact closely with the Micron staff and help guide decisions about facilities and research.

Kim Nasmyth

Kim Nasmyth studies the mechanism by which chromosomal DNA molecules are held together via entrapment within cohesin rings.

Ilan Davis

Ilan Davis works on the mechanism of mRNA transport and localized translation in Drosophila oocytes and neuromuscular junctions.

Neil Brockdorff

Neil Brockdorff's work is focused on X chromosome inactivation, the process mammals use to equalise levels of X linked genes in XX females relative to XY males.

Jordan Raff

The main goal of Jordan Raff's research is to understand how centrioles and centrosomes function at the molecular level.

Nick Proudfoot

Nick Proudfoot studies the molecular process that divides one gene from its adjacent gene partners, in particular the mechanism of transcription termination.

Liz Robertson

Liz Robertson exploits mouse genetics to investigate cell interactions underlying mammalian development and immune recognition.

Dave Sherratt


Dave Sherratt's research is aimed at understanding how DNA replication, recombination and chromosome segregation shape bacterial chromosome organization in the context of the living cell.

Shankar Srinivas

Shankar Srinivas studies cell Movement and fate during mammalian embryogenesis.

Mark Howarth

Mark Howarth's research goal is to develop new chemical and biological approaches for imaging cellular function and for diagnosis.

Jane Mellor

Jane Mellor studies how non-coding RNAs, higher order structures in chromatin and histone modifications influence the response to changes in nutrient availability and therefore longevity in S. cerevisiae.

Matthew Whitby

Matthew Whitby studies Genetic recombination and DNA repair in eukaryotes.

Rob Klose

Rob Klose studies the epigenetic regulation of chromatin function.

Oxford bioimaging

In addition to the core Micron-associated research groups, we also interact with a much larger community of researchers in the Oxford area that work in related disciplines.

Achillefs Kapanidis

Achillefs Kapanidis studies mechanisms and machines of gene expression using single-molecule biophysical methods and biochemistry, and develops single-molecule fluorescence methods, assays and DNA-based biosensors

Kay Grünewald

Kay Grünewald applies electron cryo tomography (cryo-ET) in combination with other techniques to analyze the the interaction between viral protein complexes and cellular structures in the course of the infection.

Mark Leake

Mark Leake utilizes advanced approaches of optical microscopy, such TIRF, FIONA, FRET, FRAP and FLIP to study interactions at the single molecule level that elicit and direct cellular events.

Martin Booth

Martin Booth's research centres on the development of adaptive optics, with an emphasis on applications in optical microscopy.

Weatherall Institute

The Weatherall Institute of Molecular Medicine fosters research in molecular and cell biology with direct application to the study of human disease.

BioMedIA

The Oxford Biomedical Image Analysis (BioMedIA) Laboratory conducts basic and applied research in biomedical imaging and image analysis.

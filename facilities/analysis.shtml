Image Analysis

New Biochemistry Room 00-000

 

We have a fully equipped image analysis suite in New Biochemistry. The workstations are integrated with our data storage system and an OMERO image database server (Biochemistry OMERO webclient here - internal only).

 

The analysis workstations (a mix of Windows and Linux) are equipped with the following:-

  • Fiji /ImageJ
    • ImageJ is an excellent free image analysis tool supported by NIH (and Fiji is just ImageJ with batteries included for the life sciences) ImageJ/Fiji are suitable for most image analysis tasks, certainly in 2D ( view wiki page).
  • SoftWoRx
    • for DeltaVision and OMX data; particularly wide-field deconvolution and SI reconstruction (view wiki page).
  • OMERO client
    • for accessing our OMERO image storage system (omero1.bioch.ox.ac.uk) to upload, annotate, process or download images (view wiki page).
  • Imaris
    • for 3D visualization and analysis, particularly 3D segmentation and movie-making (view wiki page).
  • Volocity
    • for 3D visualization and analysis, particularly spinning disk confocal datasets (view wiki page).
  • Matlab
    • for custom image processing and analysis solutions, as well as tools such as MicrobeTracker (view Matlab wiki page).
  • Priism & Prithon
    • free image analysis tools from the Agard & Sedat labs, where the Deltavision & and OMX technologies originated ( view wiki page).
  • Inkscape or illustrator
    • for figure editing (wiki).
  • Gimp or photoshop
    • image editors, also for creating figures (wiki).
